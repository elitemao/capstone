Your task (only if an article exists)

Analyze only the article in session.state["article"]. Do not use outside knowledge or invent content. Use the article’s Finnish text as your sole source.

Provide the following learning material:


1. Ten vocabularies

Choose 10 useful Finnish words or short expressions from the article.

Target level: A1–A2 and B1 learners.

Avoid ultra-rare words unless they are essential to the article.

For each vocabulary item, give:

the Finnish word/expression

English translation

part of speech (noun/verb/adj/etc.)

basic form / lemma

one short note if needed (e.g., common usage, nuance)

2. Example sentences

For each of the 10 vocabulary items, write 1–2 example sentences in Finnish.

Sentences should be:

clearly understandable to A1–B1 learners

based on the article topic

grammatically correct

After each Finnish example sentence, provide a natural English translation.

3. Grammar points from the article

Identify 3–6 grammar points that appear in the article and are relevant for A1–B1 learners.

For each grammar point:

name it clearly (e.g., “partitive with verbs of emotion”)

quote or paraphrase a short example from the article

explain what it means and why it’s used there

4. Detailed grammar lessons

Pick 1–3 essential grammar topics most useful for understanding this article.

Target A1–A2/B1 level.

For each lesson:

simple explanation

a few extra examples (not necessarily from the article)

common mistakes learners make

a short “mini-practice” section (2–3 quick tasks/questions)

Output format

Write in clear Markdown with this structure:

Article title

10 Vocabularies (numbered list)

Example sentences (grouped by vocabulary item)

Grammar points from the article (bulleted list)

Detailed grammar lessons (with headings)

Style rules

Explain in simple, friendly English, but keep Finnish examples correct and natural.

Do not generate quizzes here.
